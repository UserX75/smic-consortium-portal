📖 Complete Explanation: What This Investment Consortium Platform Does
Who is this for?
An investment consortium is a group of investors (institutions, family offices, high-net-worth individuals) who pool capital to invest in deals together. They need a single dashboard to track:

Portfolio performance
Deal flow
Risk exposure
Member contributions & returns

Why do they need this site?
Without a centralized system, consortium members rely on:

Scattered Excel files emailed back and forth
Manual calculations prone to error
No real-time visibility into deal pipeline
Difficulty comparing member performance
Lack of risk analytics

This prototype solves that by providing a unified, presentation-ready platform that simulates real investment data and shows how a digital tool can improve transparency, decision-making, and collaboration.

🧭 Page-by-Page Breakdown: What Each Chart Means
1. Dashboard (CEO / Board / Accounts)
Chart / Metric	What it shows	Why it matters
Total AUM	Total assets under management	Measures consortium size & growth
Portfolio Return (YTD)	Year-to-date percentage return	Quick health check
Sector Allocation Pie Chart	% of capital in Tech, Energy, Real Estate, etc.	Identifies over/under-exposure
Top 5 Holdings by Value	Largest individual investments	Concentration risk (too much in one asset)
NAV Trend Line	Net asset value over last 24 months	Shows historical growth trajectory
Treemap	Same as pie but more visual	Easy for board presentations
Waterfall Chart	Contribution of each asset to total return	Which investments are driving gains/losses
Correlation Heatmap	How assets move together	Diversification: low correlation = safer
Max Drawdown, Volatility, Beta	Risk metrics	Institutional investors demand these
Role-Based Filtering (UBAC) rationale:

CEO: Needs full picture to make strategic decisions → sees all charts & risk metrics.

Board Member: Focuses on governance & top-level performance → sees compliance score, top/bottom performers, IRR gauge.

Accounts: Needs transactional data → sees capital calls, fee calculations, recent transactions (no risk charts).

2. Deal Flow (Kanban Board)
Feature	Meaning	Why needed
Stages (Pre-screen → Due Diligence → Negotiation → Closing)	Where each deal is in the investment process	Pipeline management – know what's coming
Deal cards	Each card shows deal name, size, expected IRR, industry	Quick overview for investment committee
Pipeline metrics (Total value, avg IRR, weighted IRR)	Aggregate of all active deals	Forecast future capital needs & returns
Role filtering:
CEO can edit stages (simulate moving deals) – they own the pipeline.
Other roles view only (read-only).

3. Performance
Chart	Meaning	Why needed
Returns by Asset (bar chart)	Individual investment returns (%)	Identify winners & losers
Monthly Returns vs Benchmark	Compare portfolio to market index	Is the consortium beating the market?
Waterfall of contribution	Which assets added most $ value	Show where profits came from
Role filtering: CEO & Board see all; Accounts sees only aggregated (no individual performance for confidentiality? – but in prototype all see).

4. Risk Analytics
Metric	Meaning	Why critical
Sharpe Ratio	Return per unit of risk (higher = better)	Standard institutional metric
Max Drawdown	Largest peak-to-trough decline	Shows worst-case loss potential
VaR (Value at Risk)	Expected loss at 95% confidence	Regulatory & risk management
Correlation Heatmap	How assets move together	Diversification check
Stress tests	Hypothetical shocks (market crash, rate hike)	Prepare for downturns
Role filtering: Only CEO sees stress tests (strategic decisions). Board sees only summary.

5. Member Portal
Data	Meaning	Why needed
Committed vs Allocated capital	How much member promised vs actually invested	Track capital calls
Returns by Member	Each member's share of profits	Fair distribution
Capital call schedule	When members need to send money	Cash flow planning
Voting power (Board view)	Decision rights based on commitment	Governance

Role filtering:

Members see only their own data (in real system) – here simulated by role.
Accounts sees all capital calls to issue them.
CEO sees everything for management.

6. Scenario Analyzer (What-If)
Control	What it simulates	Why valuable
Sector return adjustments	What if Tech drops 10% but Energy rises 5%?	Strategic rebalancing
Market sentiment	Overall market boom or crash	Stress testing
Interest rate shock	How real estate/infra reacts	Hedge against rate risk
Output: New portfolio return and dollar impact.
This is a powerful presentation tool to show consortium members how the platform can help them model decisions before committing capital.

✅ How the Meetings Feature Works
Role filtering: CEO/Board see attendance analytics; all members see meetings and can RSVP.
Online attendance: Each meeting has a clickable link (simulated Google Meet, Zoom, Teams). Members can choose to attend online or in person.
RSVP simulation: Radio buttons store choice (in session state; real system would save to database).
Past recordings: Links to recordings for those who missed.

This adds significant value for a consortium with geographically dispersed members. It shows you've thought about hybrid collaboration.
